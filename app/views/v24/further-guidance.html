Apply for the hire a new apprentice payment

The hire a new apprentice payment is available for employers that take on new apprentices from 1 April 2021 to 30 September 2021. Employers can apply for the payment after they add new apprentices to their apprenticeship service account.

Did the apprentice join your organisation from 1 April 2021?

Employers will receive £3,000 for new apprentices of any age who have an employment start date of 1 April 2021 to 30 September 2021.They must have an apprenticeship start date of 1 April 2021 to 30 November 2021.

Employers are also able to apply for new apprentices who they recruited under the Kickstart scheme.

Did the apprentice join your organisation before 1 April 2021?

The application deadline for these apprentices has closed.

When will you get paid?

We’ll send the payment in 2 equal instalments for each apprentice.
We’ll send the:

  • first payment after an apprentice completes 90 days of their apprenticeship
  • second payment after an apprentice completes 365 days of their apprenticeship

Payments are sent by Bacs on the 14th working day of the month. It usually takes 3 working days to process.

We cannot send any payments until we’ve received and verified {{_orgNamePlural}} organisation and finance details. This could take up to 80 days and we may contact someone from {{_orgName}}.

What can you use the payment for?

{{_orgName}} can spend it on anything to support their organisation’s costs. {{_orgName}} does not have to pay it back.

Who is eligible?

{{_orgName}} can only apply for new apprentices who joined your organisation from 1 April 2021 to 30 September 2021.

{{_orgName}} cannot apply for an existing employee who joined your organisation before 1 April 2021, even if they started an apprenticeship after this date.

The payment is in addition to the existing £1,000 an employer will already get for taking on an apprentice who is either:

  • aged 16 to 18 years old
  • under 25 and has an education, health and care plan or has been in the care of their local authority
